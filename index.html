Focus: Social Neuroscience

About Me.

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student at Dartmouth College, working in the COSAN Lab with Professor Luke Chang. My research examines how the human brain responds to naturalistic stimuli (e.g. movie watching) and how these neural processes enable effective information transmission and foster successful communication in real-world contexts. To address these questions, I integrate multimodal data—including fMRI and fNIRS (with hyperscanning), behavioral measures, physiological signals, and computational models.

Education.

I received my M.S. from the State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Neuroscience and Learning at Beijing Normal University, where I was advised by Professor Guosheng Ding. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Applied Psychology from Nanjing Normal University.

Interests.

I am broadly interested in social interaction, human–machine interaction, and affective computing. To understand these processes, I enjoy working with complex, multimodal datasets, applying quantitative and computational methods to uncover and visualize patterns, interpret results, and generate insights that bridge human behavior with real-world applications.

Personal.

I was born and raised in Jiangsu Province, China. After moving to the U.S., I’ve split my time between Hanover, New Hampshire, and the greater Seattle area. Outside of research, I enjoy playing tennis, photography, hiking, and traveling to explore new places.
